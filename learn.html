🧠 Music & The Study Brain

The science behind why the right music makes you study better — and which type to pick.

🎼

The Mozart Effect

Studies in the 1990s suggested that listening to Mozart temporarily boosted spatial reasoning scores. While debated, the finding launched decades of research into music and cognition.

🧘

Stress & Cortisol

Slow-tempo music (under 60 BPM) measurably reduces cortisol — the stress hormone. This is the science behind our Sleep and Chill playlists.

⚡

Dopamine Release

Music you enjoy triggers dopamine — the same chemical released during rewards. This is why the right track makes a study session feel effortless and motivating.

💡 5 Tips to Get More from Study Music

  1. Use headphones, not speakers

    Headphones create an auditory bubble that dramatically reduces ambient distractions.

  2. Keep volume at 50–60%

    Loud music increases cognitive load. Moderate volume is the sweet spot for retention.

  3. Match mood to task, not preference

    Your favourite energetic playlist might be wrong for a quiet reading session.

  4. Use the same playlist consistently

    Pavlovian conditioning — your brain learns to enter focus mode when it hears familiar tracks.

  5. Take silent breaks every 90 minutes

    Silence during breaks helps consolidate memory. Don't music through your rest.

❌ Genres to Avoid While Studying

  • 🎤 Pop with lyrics — triggers language processing areas
  • 📻 Talk radio / podcasts — competes with reading
  • 🔊 Heavy metal — elevated cortisol in most listeners
  • 🎵 New songs — novelty causes attention shifts

✅ Genres That Work Best

  • 🎹 Instrumental
    • Piano solos
    • Guitar ambient
  • 🎺 Jazz
    • Modal jazz
    • Bossa nova
  • 🌿 Nature sounds
  • 🎶 Lo-fi hip-hop
